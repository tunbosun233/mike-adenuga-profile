Globacom

Globacom, commonly known as Glo, is a Nigerian telecommunications company providing mobile, broadband, and enterprise network services. It is one of the largest telecom operators in Nigeria and a key competitor in West Africa’s communications market, recognized for pioneering innovations in mobile data and international connectivity.

Key facts

  • Founded: 2003

  • Founder: Mike Adenuga

  • Headquarters: Lagos, Nigeria

  • Subscribers: Over 60 million (approx., 2023)

  • Operations: Nigeria, Ghana, Benin, Côte d’Ivoire

Background and growth

Founded by Nigerian entrepreneur Mike Adenuga in 2003, Globacom launched with a vision to make telecommunications affordable and locally owned. The company entered a market dominated by international firms and quickly gained traction through competitive pricing and customer-focused services. Its "Rule Your World" slogan emphasized empowerment through connectivity.

Network and technology

Globacom was the first telecom operator in Nigeria to build an independent fiber-optic backbone, known as Glo 1, linking West Africa to Europe via an undersea cable. This infrastructure significantly enhanced internet bandwidth and reliability in the region. Glo provides 2G, 3G, and 4G LTE services and has announced ongoing plans toward 5G readiness.

Market presence and impact

Globacom’s expansion beyond Nigeria into neighboring countries—Benin, Ghana, and Côte d’Ivoire—cemented its position as a pan-African player. The company is a major contributor to Nigeria’s digital economy and has promoted local employment, entertainment sponsorships, and tech innovation, including support for startups and data-driven services.

Sponsorships and brand influence

Glo is known for its high-profile sponsorships in sports and entertainment, including partnerships with the Nigerian Football Federation, CAF tournaments, and music awards. Its endorsements by African celebrities have made it one of the most recognizable consumer brands on the continent.
